# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary**: Clinic practice managers, GP owners, and practice nurses in Australian healthcare clinics.

**Context**: Busy healthcare professionals researching automation solutions for their practices. They visit when overwhelmed by administrative burden, facing RACGP compliance requirements, or seeking efficiency improvements. They need quick, clear answers about whether ClinicIQ can solve their specific problems.

**Job-to-be-done**: Rapidly assess if ClinicIQ offers relevant automation tools for their clinic's pain points, understand what RACGP compliance solutions are available, and determine whether to engage further (contact, newsletter, or direct inquiry).

## Product Purpose

ClinicIQ Solutions provides AI automation tools, productivity calculators, and healthcare templates specifically designed for Australian GP clinics. Built by a Registered Nurse, the platform addresses real clinical workflow challenges — practice management, compliance documentation, and administrative efficiency. Success is a practice manager finding a relevant tool and understanding its value within 60 seconds of landing on the site.

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: Professional, Trustworthy, Modern

**Voice**: Clear, direct, clinically precise — notsalesy or overly casual. Speaks healthcare professional to healthcare professional.

**Emotional goals**: Reassurance that ClinicIQ understands clinical workflows, confidence that tools are built by someone with real healthcare experience, trust that Australian/RACGP context is respected.

## Anti-references

- **Generic teal-and-white healthcare sites**: Avoid the clichéd medical aesthetic (stock photos of smiling doctors, teal gradients, sterile corporate feel)
- **SaaS startup clichés**: No hero-metric templates, identical card grids, "revolutionary AI" marketing fluff, or exaggerated claims
- **Overly tech-forward interfaces**: This audience values clinical authority over startup aesthetics — avoid dark mode developer tools or overly abstract visuals

## Design Principles

1. **Clinical authority first**: Every design choice should signal healthcare expertise and reliability, not tech trend-chasing
2. **Clarity over cleverness**: Information architecture prioritizes rapid comprehension — practice managers don't have time for mystery navigation or buried content
3. **Australian context matters**: RACGP compliance, local healthcare systems, and nurse-led design are core differentiators — feature them prominently
4. **Subtle depth, not distraction**: Gradients, shadows, and motion should create sophistication without overwhelming clinical content
5. **Mobile as primary context**: Many practice managers browse on phones between patients — mobile experience is not an afterthought

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** minimum standard for all content
- **Semantic HTML**: Proper heading hierarchy, ARIA labels on interactive elements, screen reader support
- **Keyboard navigation**: All interactive elements accessible via keyboard, logical tab order
- **Color contrast**: 4.5:1 ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text
- **Reduced motion**: Respects `prefers-reduced-motion` — animations disabled when requested
- **Font loading**: Content visible immediately (font-display: swap fallback) to prevent FOUT-related accessibility issues
